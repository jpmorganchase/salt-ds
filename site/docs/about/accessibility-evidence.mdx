Salt targets WCAG 2.2 Level AA. Accessibility is assessed at the level of a
component and its documented use, then re-assessed in the product where it is
composed. We do not currently claim formal conformance for the full Salt
library.

The pilot below makes the evidence boundary explicit. It records what automated
tests cover, which manual assistive-technology scenarios are pending, known
limitations, an owner and the next review date. The
[machine-readable manifest](https://github.com/jpmorganchase/salt-ds/blob/main/accessibility/component-evidence.json)
is the source of truth.

## Evidence vocabulary

- **Target** identifies the guideline and level a component is designed to meet.
  It is a goal, not a test result.
- **Tested** means the linked automated or manual evidence passed for the exact
  behavior and environment recorded. It does not cover unlisted behavior.
- **Supported** means the maintainers have current, dated evidence for the
  documented browser and assistive-technology combination.
- **Exception** is a known gap against the target. Each exception must describe
  its impact, owner and review date.
- **Formally conformant** is reserved for a defined scope that has completed an
  appropriate conformance assessment. None of the pilot entries makes this
  claim.

## Manual testing targets

Salt targets these combinations for manual testing. A target becomes tested or
supported only when the component manifest links a dated result.

| Operating system | Browser | Assistive technology |
| ---------------- | ------- | -------------------- |
| Windows          | Firefox | NVDA                 |
| Windows          | Chrome  | JAWS                 |
| macOS            | Safari  | VoiceOver            |

All three combinations are currently **not tested** for the pilot components in
the public evidence record. Automated axe, keyboard and DOM assertions are
useful evidence, but they do not substitute for operating the component with a
screen reader.

## Pilot components

### Spinner

- **Status:** Tested automated behavior; not formally conformant.
- **WCAG evidence:** 1.1.1 Non-text Content and 4.1.3 Status Messages.
- **Automated evidence:** Accessible names, recurring progress announcements,
  completion, disabled announcements and unmount cleanup are covered in the
  [Spinner accessibility test](https://github.com/jpmorganchase/salt-ds/blob/main/packages/core/src/__tests__/__e2e__/spinner/Spinner.accessibility.cy.tsx).
- **Manual evidence:** NVDA with Firefox, JAWS with Chrome and VoiceOver with
  Safari are pending for progress and completion announcements.
- **Known exceptions:** None recorded.
- **Limitation:** Programmatic announcement assertions do not establish how a
  screen reader presents the messages.
- **Owner and review:** Salt maintainers; reviewed July 12, 2026; review by
  October 12, 2026.

### Number Input

- **Status:** Tested automated behavior; not formally conformant.
- **WCAG evidence:** 1.3.1 Info and Relationships, 2.1.1 Keyboard, 3.3.2 Labels
  or Instructions and 4.1.2 Name, Role, Value.
- **Automated evidence:** The
  [accessibility test](https://github.com/jpmorganchase/salt-ds/blob/main/packages/core/src/__tests__/__e2e__/number-input/NumberInput.accessibility.cy.tsx)
  covers role, name, description, bounds, validity and decorative controls. The
  [interaction test](https://github.com/jpmorganchase/salt-ds/blob/main/packages/core/src/__tests__/__e2e__/number-input/NumberInput.cy.tsx)
  covers keyboard changes.
- **Manual evidence:** NVDA with Firefox, JAWS with Chrome and VoiceOver with
  Safari are pending for labels, help text, value changes and validation.
- **Known exceptions:** None recorded.
- **Limitation:** Product labels, instructions and validation content remain the
  product team's responsibility and require testing in context.
- **Owner and review:** Salt maintainers; reviewed July 12, 2026; review by
  October 12, 2026.

### Mega Menu

- **Status:** Exception. The listed automated behavior passed; the focus-guard
  exception below remains open; not formally conformant.
- **WCAG evidence:** 1.3.1 Info and Relationships, 2.1.1 Keyboard, 2.4.3 Focus
  Order and 4.1.2 Name, Role, Value.
- **Automated evidence:** The
  [accessibility test](https://github.com/jpmorganchase/salt-ds/blob/main/packages/core/src/__tests__/__e2e__/mega-menu/MegaMenu.accessibility.cy.tsx)
  covers landmarks, names, lists, state, current items and enforced axe checks
  other than the documented focus-guard rule. The
  [keyboard test](https://github.com/jpmorganchase/salt-ds/blob/main/packages/core/src/__tests__/__e2e__/mega-menu/MegaMenu.keyboardNavigation.cy.tsx)
  covers trigger, panel, responsive and nested-control navigation.
- **Manual evidence:** NVDA with Firefox, JAWS with Chrome and VoiceOver with
  Safari are pending for identifying, opening, navigating and closing the menu.
- **Known exception:** With a panel open, axe 4.11 reports
  `aria-hidden-focus` for Floating UI's `aria-hidden`, tabbable focus guards.
  The rule is explicitly excluded while its assistive-technology impact is
  assessed. This relates to WCAG 4.1.2; Salt maintainers own the open exception
  and will review it by October 12, 2026.
- **Limitation:** Automated keyboard and axe checks do not establish reading
  order or announcement quality in assistive technology.
- **Owner and review:** Salt maintainers; reviewed July 12, 2026; review by
  October 12, 2026.

## Maintaining evidence

The accessibility manifest is reviewed at least quarterly and whenever a pilot
component's accessibility behavior changes. `yarn check:accessibility-evidence`
fails when a review is stale, required fields or manual matrix entries are
missing, evidence links do not resolve, or a pilot is marked formally conformant
without an assessment. It runs as part of `yarn verify:lint` in continuous
integration.

Manual results should be added only after the named scenario is run with the
exact operating system, browser and assistive technology in the manifest.
Record the date and outcome, summarize relevant observations without personal or
confidential information, and create an owned exception for a failure. If a
detailed record cannot be made public, publish only the safe attestation fields
in the manifest: combination, scenario, outcome, date and owner. Never infer a
manual pass from an automated test.
